Taraz

South Kazakhstan

General Information:

Region: Jambyl Region

Founded: 36 BCE

Population(2009): 406,262


Brief Summary:

Taraz, a.k.a Talas to foreigners, is one of the oldest cities in Kazakhstan. The countdown started when a fortress was built in the area during the Battle of Zhizhi in 36 BCE. Taraz was given its name in 568 BCE by Menander Protector who named it "Talas".

The medieval city was a major trade center along the Silk Road. Taraz secured a place in history by virtue of the Battle of Talas (751 CE) which was fought between forces of the Chinese Tang dynasty and the Arab Abbasid Califate. One of the indirect outcomes of the battle was the introduction of paper to the West via Arabs capturing Chinese paper makers.


Medieval Taraz

Since the discovery of the land, Taraz was ruled by:

  • Persion Samanids (late 9th century)
  • Turkic Karakhanids (10th-12th centuries)
  • Mongols (1220-1334)
  • Kazakh tribes and Kazakh Khanate (~1513-1723)
  • Dzungars (1723-1755)
  • Qing Rule (1755-19th century)
  • Khanate of Kokand (1856-1864)
  • Russian Rule (1864-1991)

In December of 1991, Kazakhstan declared independence from USSR making Taraz the official land of Kazakhstan.


Modern Taraz

Useful info for visitors:

  1. One of the sights to visit is Aisha Bibi Mausoleum located 18 km west of Taraz.
  2. Taraz has a semi-desert climate. It has a mean January temperature of -3 °C and a mean July temperature of 25.5 °C.
  3. Taraz is hosting the football team FC Taraz that plays in the Kazakhstan Premier League.
